# Slot RTP & Volatility Spec

> Status: normative for the 7 Goldwave/Aureus slot **engines** (`apps/api/src/games/engines/<game>/math.ts`).
> Scope: `cosmic`, `dragon`, `inferno`, `kirin`, `leviathan`, `phoenix`, `royal`. The two non-slot
> originals (`plinko`, `wheel`) are out of scope. This document defines the enterprise-norm targets the
> engine math is tuned to; the realized numbers are MEASURED by each game's `simulate.ts` probe and
> recorded in the retune PR.

## 1. Certified RTP band

Modern online slots cluster their **certified, headline RTP at ~96%** — the figure a lab certifies and the
operator publishes. Industry guidance treats 96% as the "average" online-slot RTP, and reputable studios
publish flagships in a tight band around it. Pragmatic Play, for example, ships most flagship titles as an
**"Enhanced RTP Ladder"** of separately certified models — **96.50% / 95.50% / 94.50%** (some top settings at
96.51–96.71%) — and the operator chooses which certified model to run. Reputable high-end sweepstakes
operators (Stake.us, McLuck, Pulsz) run the **higher** configurations, not the cut-down ones.

**Goldwave policy:** every slot runs a single high-end certified model in the **95.5–96.5%** band, **default
96.0%**. Each engine declares its target as `CERTIFIED_RTP_BPS` (basis points; `9600` = 96.00%). The retune
holds every game to its declared `CERTIFIED_RTP_BPS` and calibrates `PAYOUT_SCALAR_BPS` until the **measured**
RTP from `simulate.ts` is within **±0.3%** of that target.

| | bps | %     |
|----------------|------|-------|
| Band floor     | 9550 | 95.5% |
| Default target | 9600 | 96.0% |
| Band ceiling   | 9650 | 96.5% |

## 2. RTP is fixed per game and bet-INDEPENDENT (compliance red line)

RTP is a **property of the game's math model**, certified once by an independent lab (GLI, eCOGRA, iTech Labs)
and **identical for every player at every bet level**. Under **GLI-19** (Standards for Interactive Gaming
Systems), every game outcome is generated by the central gaming platform's RNG and must be **independent of
the player's device, history, and stake** — outcomes are determined **solely by the RNG**, and a game must
**not adapt its payout** to who is playing, how much they have wagered, how much they have won or lost, or
which coin/credit type they are using. Identical odds, every spin, every player.

Concretely, for these engines:

- The engine math (reel weights, paytables, feature tables, `PAYOUT_SCALAR_BPS`) is **frozen** and produces
  one fixed RTP. The spin result is a pure function of the committed provable-fairness seed + nonce.
- **No per-bet, per-player, or per-history win-rate variation may be added to an engine.** This is a hard
  red line. Where the platform exposes an operator `rtpBps` override, it is applied **outside** the engine,
  at the settlement layer (`games.service.ts`), and never feeds back into the engine's outcome generation —
  the engine's odds are the same regardless of any override. The retune does not touch that path.
- RTP is **emergent and measured**, never asserted from a single constant. Every table change is re-measured
  with the game's seeded Monte-Carlo probe (`simulate.ts`) before it is accepted.

## 3. Volatility tiers

Two slots can share the same 96% RTP and feel completely different: the same return can be delivered as
thousands of tiny wins or as rare, large ones. That shape is **volatility**, characterized by four levers:

- **Hit frequency** — share of spins returning any win (`win > 0`).
- **Win-size distribution** — how returned wins split across `<1×`, `1–5×`, `5–20×`, `20–100×`, `100×+`.
- **Max win** — the headline capped top multiple (× total bet).
- **Feature frequency** — how often the headline feature (free spins / hold-and-spin / bonus) triggers.

We report, per game, the share of spins paying each band: **0× (loss), <1×, 1–5×, 5–20×, 20–100×, 100×+**.
(The probes bucket coarsely as `dead / <1× / 1–10× / 10–100× / 100×+`; the PR records the probe buckets
verbatim alongside this finer reporting intent.)

| Tier   | Hit frequency        | Win-size shape                                   | Max win        | Feature frequency |
|--------|----------------------|--------------------------------------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| LOW    | ~40–50%              | most wins 0.5–2×; 5×+ rare                        | ~hundreds ×    | ~1 in 80–120      |
| MEDIUM | ~25–35%              | mostly 1–5×, occasional 5–20×                    | ~1,000–5,000×  | ~1 in 120–180     |
| HIGH   | meaningful-hit ~18–25% | fat tail 5–50×+, big wins rare                 | ~5,000–50,000× | ~1 in 180–300     |

A **MEDIUM-HIGH** working tier sits between MEDIUM and HIGH: hit ~24–32%, occasional 5–20× with a real
20–100×+ tail, max ~3,000–8,000×, feature ~1 in 150–220. It is used for the two games whose mechanic
(a 25-line dragon slot; a hold-and-spin) naturally lands more base wins than a pure HIGH ways game.

"Meaningful-hit" for HIGH excludes sub-1× consolation returns: a HIGH game may still light up cells often,
but the share of spins that return **more than the stake** is what the ~18–25% band refers to.

## 4. Per-game tier assignment

Each game is assigned a tier from its mechanic, with target hit frequency and target feature frequency. All
seven target `CERTIFIED_RTP_BPS = 9600` (96.0%). The engine is tuned **toward** these bands and the achieved
numbers are measured per game in the PR; where a measured value lands just outside a band it is documented.

| Game        | Mechanic                                    | Tier        | Target hit freq | Target feature freq        | Max-win intent  |
|-------------|---------------------------------------------|-------------|-----------------|----------------------------|-----------------|
| `phoenix`   | 5×3 / 243-ways, sticky-ORB free spins       | HIGH        | ~18–24%         | free spins ~1 in 180–280   | ≥ 5,000×        |
| `cosmic`    | 5×3, 25-line, rising-mult FS + instant bonus| HIGH        | ~18–24%         | free spins ~1 in 180–280   | ≥ 5,000×        |
| `kirin`     | 5×4, 25-line, FS + bonus + 4-tier jackpot   | HIGH        | ~20–25%         | free spins ~1 in 180–280; jackpot rare | ≥ 5,000× |
| `leviathan` | 6×5 ways, tumbling, rising-tide FS + Kraken | HIGH (flagship) | ~20–25%     | free spins ~1 in 180–300   | toward cap ~20,000× |
| `dragon`    | 5×3, 25-line, rising-mult free spins        | MEDIUM-HIGH | ~25–31%         | free spins ~1 in 150–220   | ~3,000–8,000×   |
| `inferno`   | 5×4, 25-line, hold-and-spin (lock & respin) | MEDIUM-HIGH | ~26–34%         | hold-and-spin ~1 in 110–200| ~3,000–8,000×   |
| `royal`     | 5×3 ways, rising-mult free spins            | MEDIUM      | ~26–33%         | free spins ~1 in 120–180   | ~1,000–5,000×   |

### Notes on individual mechanics

- **phoenix / cosmic** — pure HIGH 5×3 games. The cheap-filler floor is trimmed so the share of spins that
  *return more than the stake* drops into the HIGH band, and the premium top-of-table 5-of-a-kind pays are
  steepened so the rare wins are large. Free-spin triggers are made rarer (HIGH feature band) and the
  multiplier ramp / sticky-ORB carries the tail.
- **kirin** — HIGH line game whose headline tail is partly carried by the four-tier fixed jackpot (paid
  verbatim, never RTP-scaled) plus a fat premium line tail. The jackpot strike is a rare event by design.
- **leviathan** — the flagship. Retuned away from a ~93–95% profile with a heavy sub-1× dribble (≈46% of
  spins returning <1×) into a clean HIGH profile: far fewer hollow sub-1× wins, a real meaningful-hit band,
  and a fat premium/cascade/tide tail reaching toward the 20,000× cap, all at the certified 96.0%.
- **dragon / inferno** — MEDIUM-HIGH. Their mechanics naturally land more base wins; they keep a livelier hit
  frequency with a strengthened 5–100×+ tail rather than the leaner HIGH floor.
- **royal** — the MEDIUM reference: steady ~30% hit, a 1–5× core, and a 1,000–5,000× capped tail.

## 5. Bonus-feature invariant (held throughout)

Independent of tier, every slot's bonus/free-spins feature must reward the player **more than base play** —
both **more often** (higher hit frequency) and **more per spin** (higher mean win), measured on the
feature's reel weights vs the base reel weights at multiplier 1. This is asserted by
`apps/api/src/games/engines/bonus-winrate.test.ts` (and, for leviathan, in its own `engine.test.ts`). The
retune preserves it for all seven games.
